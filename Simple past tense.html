Simple Past Tense

The simple past tense is used to describe an action that happened in the past and is no longer happening.

Definition

The simple past tense is defined as "an action happened in the past (before present time) and there is no longer happening of this action".As you know, this tense is very important in communicating to explain past events.You can also pay attention to several sentences spoken by film actors and actresses or singers, they convey things that have been on their minds and feelings that have been happening for a long time.

Structure

The structure of the simple past tense is as follows:

  • Assertive sentence: Subject + past form of verb (verb + ed/d)
  • Negative sentence: Subject + did not + root verb (present form of verb)
  • Interrogative sentence: Did + Sub + root verb (present form)

Examples

For example this is the regular and irregular sentence in simple past tense.

They played cricket in the field.

We went for a movie.

We saw a beautiful sunset at the beach.

They watched the movie last night.
